## 🧪 Phase 3 – Digital Forensics (DFIR)

In this phase I will concentrate on digital forensics and incident response, connecting it to my formal background in Forensic Sciences.

### Self-Directed Digital Forensics Training (Free Courses)

I am building a structured foundation in digital forensics through free online courses from reputable universities and cybersecurity organizations:

#### 1. Digital Forensics – OpenLearn (The Open University)
Free introductory course covering the core principles of digital forensics, including the investigation process, handling of digital evidence, and basic analysis of storage devices and file systems. Ideal to connect traditional forensic science with modern digital evidence.

#### 2. Introduction to Digital Forensics – Alison
Free online course that explains the stages of a digital forensic investigation, types of digital evidence, acquisition and preservation techniques, and reporting. Includes a completion certificate and practical case‑based examples.

#### 3. Digital Forensics Essentials (DFE) – EC‑Council
Beginner‑level course that introduces key digital forensics concepts, investigation methodologies, common tools, and real‑world use cases. Designed as an entry point into DFIR and cybersecurity analyst roles.

#### 4. DFIR Training Collections – DFIR.training & DFIR Diva
Curated lists of free and low‑cost DFIR training resources, including short courses and labs on topics such as OSINT, memory forensics, Windows and mobile forensics, and incident response. I use these platforms to select focused, hands‑on modules that complement my formal forensic science background.

**Practical goals for this phase:**

- Build small case studies (e.g. disk image analysis, basic timeline analysis).  
- Write investigation notes and reports in this repository.  
- Develop simple scripts/tools to support forensic workflows.
